Does Mauna Loa Have Lava Flow?

On Sunday, November 27, 2022, Mauna Loa erupted for the first time since 1984. That eruption has stopped for now. To follow volcano updates, please see the Mauna Loa Eruption page.

Is There Any Lava Flowing At Kīlauea?

Though continuously active, Kīlauea Volcano’s flowing lava isn’t always at the surface. Kīlauea last erupted in 2018, and occasionally the glow from the lava lake is visible—or a surface flow of lava—but you will need to check with your tour advisor or local weather forecast.

When And Where Was The Last Major Eruption?

On November 27th, 2022, Mauna Loa erupted. Mauna Loa is the world’s largest active volcano. There has never been a better time to book a volcano tour with us because Kīlauea also has visible lava. In 2018 there was a volcanic eruption on the Big Island at Kīlauea, which began on May 3, 2018, and lasted until September 4, 2018. Haleakalā has erupted at least ten times in the past 1,000 years; according to scientific records, the last eruption occurred sometime between 1480 and 1600.
